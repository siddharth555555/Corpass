# Corpass: The Unified Corporate Procurement & Asset Management Platform

## Overview
Corpass is a comprehensive B2B marketplace designed to streamline how corporate entities procure supplies and manage their internal assets. It bridges the gap between verified corporate sellers and enterprise buyers, offering end-to-end workflows from product discovery and negotiation to order fulfillment, stock tracking, and internal asset lifecycle management.

## 🏢 Buyer Features

### 1. Marketplace Discovery
- **Comprehensive Catalog**: Browse products across 12+ categories including Office Supplies, Furniture, IT & Electronics, SaaS, and Professional Services.
- **Advanced Filtering**: Search and filter by category, brand, and sorting preferences (e.g., Price Low to High).
- **Verified Suppliers**: View supplier details, locations, and company profiles directly on product cards.

### 2. Inquiry & Negotiation (RFQs)
- **Direct Inquiries**: Send direct messages to sellers to ask for quotes, feasibility, or availability on specific products.
- **Counter-Offer System**: Buyers and sellers can negotiate prices directly within the platform. Sellers can propose a custom "Response Price," which the buyer can review, accept, or decline.
- **Threaded Messaging**: Contextual messaging threads tied to specific inquiries or orders for clear communication.

### 3. Order Management
- **Direct Checkout**: Place orders directly from the marketplace for fixed-price items or items with agreed-upon quotes.
- **Order Tracking**: Track the lifecycle of an order (`PENDING` -> `CONFIRMED` -> `SHIPPED` -> `DELIVERED` or `CANCELLED`).
- **Invoicing**: Receive and acknowledge manual invoices generated by sellers. Dispute resolution functionality is built-in.

### 4. Internal Asset Management
- **Automated Logging**: When an order is marked as `DELIVERED`, the items are automatically added to the company's internal Asset Management system.
- **Condition Tracking**: Track the physical condition of assets across multiple states (Perfect, Good, Fair, Poor, Broken) simultaneously for a single product type.
- **Manual Asset Entry**: IT and Admin teams can manually add and track existing company assets.

## 🏪 Seller Features

### 1. Product Catalog Management
- **List New Products**: Add products or services with detailed descriptions, categorizations, and unit of measure definitions (e.g., Box of 50, Per Year, Litre).
- **Flexible Pricing Models**: Choose between "Fixed Price" and "Contact for Quote" (for variable or large-scale B2B orders).
- **Delivery Constraints**: Define Minimum Order Quantities (MOQ), Minimum Order Values, and expected delivery timelines.

### 2. Dedicated Stock Management
- **Live Inventory Tracking**: A dedicated dashboard (`/dashboard/seller/stock`) to monitor inventory levels across the entire catalog.
- **Automated Deductions**: Stock is automatically deducted when an order is moved to the `SHIPPED` state.
- **Low Stock Alerts**: Visual indicators highlight items that are low in stock (below MOQ) or entirely out of stock, alerting both the seller and potential buyers.
- **Rapid Updates**: Quickly edit and correct stock quantities without navigating through complex edit screens.

### 3. Order Fulfillment & Invoicing
- **Lifecycle Control**: Sellers control the fulfillment pipeline, moving orders from `CONFIRMED` to `SHIPPED` and finally `DELIVERED`.
- **Manual Invoicing**: Generate custom invoices (`INV-XXXX`) for off-platform agreements or specialized professional services.
- **Delivery Range Configuration**: Set operational delivery radiuses (e.g., Local 100km, State-wide, Pan-India).

### 4. Direct B2B Communication
- **Centralized Inbox**: Respond to buyer inquiries, negotiate pricing, and answer pre-sales questions from a unified messaging interface.

## 🔒 Platform Security & Architecture

### Tech Stack
- **Frontend**: Next.js (React), Tailwind CSS, TypeScript
- **Backend**: NestJS, Prisma ORM
- **Database**: MySQL
- **Authentication**: JWT-based role authentication

### Security Features
- **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)**: Strict separation between `BUYER` and `SELLER` roles, ensuring users can only access endpoints and data relevant to their profile.
- **Ownership Verification**: Database queries strictly verify resource ownership (e.g., a seller can only update stock for their own products, buyers can only view their own orders).
- **Transaction Safety**: Complex operations (like order creation + initial messaging, or shipping + stock deduction) are wrapped in atomic database transactions (`prisma.$transaction`) to prevent data corruption.
