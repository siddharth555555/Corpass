Phase 1: Business Foundation

Before writing a single line of code, lock in the business fundamentals. These decisions will determine everything that follows.

Phase Progress 0%
1

Define Your Niche

Don't launch with 12 categories. Pick 2–3 verticals where you have seller relationships.

Tip: Office Supplies + IT Hardware are the easiest entry points because every company needs them, and sellers are abundant.
2

Seller Outreach

You need sellers on the platform BEFORE buyers arrive. The chicken comes before the egg.

Key Ask: "Give me your catalog and prices. I'll bring you buyers. No upfront fee."
3

Buyer Validation

Confirm that real companies actually want to buy through your platform, not just directly from the seller.

Validation Criteria: If they say "I just call my existing supplier," you need a stronger value proposition. If they say "I compare 3 quotes every time," you have a fit.
4

Pricing & Revenue Model

How does Corpass make money? Define this before launch so sellers know the rules.

Commission Model
Take 3–5% of every successful order. Sellers pay nothing upfront. Scales with volume.
Subscription Model
₹499/month for basic listing. ₹1999/month for featured placement, analytics, priority support.
5

Legal & Compliance

GST invoicing, terms of service, and data privacy are non-negotiable in B2B.

6

Brand & Domain

Your first impression is your landing page. Make it count.

Don't overbuild: A clean landing page with a form that emails you is enough for the first 2 weeks. You don't need a custom website before the product is live.

Phase 1 Output

When you complete Phase 1, you should have:

✓ 3–5 committed sellers with product catalogs
✓ 1–2 buyer companies with LOI
✓ Revenue model defined (commission + subscription)
✓ Legal entity + GSTIN + bank account
✓ Domain + landing page + business email
✓ Terms of Service and Privacy Policy drafted

Phase 3: User Onboarding

How to get buyers and sellers from first visit to first transaction. Each persona has a different journey.

Seller Onboarding Flow

Sellers are the supply side. You need them BEFORE buyers. The onboarding must be fast and low-friction.

1

Invitation

Send a personal WhatsApp/email to the seller with a unique invite link. Don't make them find you.

"Hi [Name], I'm launching Corpass — a platform for corporate buyers. I'd love to list your [Category] products. Here's your registration link: [URL]"
2

Registration

Seller fills: name, login ID, email, mobile, password, address, city, pincode.

Then selects "I am a Seller" and adds:

  • GSTIN (mandatory)
  • Delivery Range (Local/Hyper-local/Shipping)
  • Delivery Cities / Pincodes
3

Catalog Setup

Guide the seller to add their first 5 products. Offer to do it FOR them if they're busy.

Pro tip: Ask the seller to send their product catalog as a PDF or Excel. You upload the first 5 products yourself to demonstrate the platform. Sellers add the rest later.
4

Stock Configuration

Seller sets stock quantities for each product. Explain that stock auto-deducts on ship.

Emphasize: "Keep this updated. If you show 100 in stock but only have 20, you'll get bad reviews."

5

Pricing Explanation

Explain the two price types:

  • Fixed Price: Buyer can order directly. Good for standard products.
  • Contact for Quote: Buyer must inquire first. Good for custom/services.
6

First Order Activation

The seller's first order is the activation moment. Call them immediately when it comes in.

Phone call script: "Hi [Name], you just got your first order on Corpass for [Product]. Log in to confirm it. I'm here to help if you get stuck."

Buyer Onboarding Flow

Buyers are the demand side. They won't come unless you have products. Onboard them AFTER sellers are ready.

1

Direct Outreach

Call the procurement head personally. Don't send a mass email. B2B is relationship-driven.

"Hi [Name], I've built a platform where you can get quotes from 5 verified suppliers for your office supplies in 10 minutes. Want to see a demo?"
2

Live Demo

Do a 15-minute screen share. Show them the marketplace, inquiry system, and order tracking. Let them send a test inquiry to a real seller.

The goal is to get a real RFQ sent during the demo.

3

Registration

Buyer registers with personal details AND company details:

  • Company Name
  • Company Address
  • Company Type
  • Employee Count

This company profile is shared across all employees who register later.

4

First Inquiry

Guide the buyer to send their first inquiry. This is the "aha" moment.

Pre-populate the inquiry form with a product they actually need. Don't make them search from scratch.

5

Order & Payment

When a seller responds, guide the buyer to convert the inquiry to an order. Walk them through payment recording.

Explain: "You pay the seller directly via bank transfer. Then record the UTR here so both of you have a shared record."

6

Asset Discovery

After delivery, show the buyer their auto-logged asset. This is the unique value prop.

Re-engagement hook: "Remember that 500-chair order from 3 months ago? They're all logged in your asset dashboard. You know exactly where they are."

Admin Onboarding

You (the founder) are the first admin. You need to know how to operate the platform before anyone else uses it.

1

Admin Account Creation

Run the seed script to create the admin:

node scripts/seed-admin.js

Default: loginId = "admin", password = "admin" (change immediately after first login).

2

Dashboard Familiarization

Log in to the admin portal and review:

  • Total users, orders, revenue stats
  • Recent activity feed
  • User list (filter by role)
  • Order list (filter by status)
  • Support tickets
3

Support Ticket Workflow

When a user raises a support ticket:

  • You receive a notification in the admin portal
  • View the ticket details and user info
  • Contact the user via email/phone (off-platform for now)
  • Update the ticket status manually (future: in-app response)
4

Daily Operations Checklist

Every morning, check:

  • New registrations (are they real or spam?)
  • Pending orders (are sellers responding?)
  • Open support tickets
  • Disputed invoices or payments
  • Low-stock alerts (notify sellers proactively)

Launch Principles

"Do Things That Don't Scale"
For the first 30 days, manually onboard every seller and buyer. Call them. Help them upload products. The platform should feel personal, not self-service.
"One Transaction is Worth 100 Features"
Don't build more features until 10 real transactions have completed. Every feature you build before that is a guess. Let users tell you what they need.
"Sellers Before Buyers, Always"
A buyer who sees an empty marketplace leaves forever. A seller with no buyers just waits. You can simulate demand by being the buyer's first inquiry yourself.
"Commission is Your Validation"
Don't charge subscription until you have 10+ active sellers. Start with commission-only. When sellers are making money, they'll happily pay for premium features.

Red Flags to Watch

  • ⚠ Sellers uploading catalogs but getting zero inquiries → Your buyer outreach is too weak.
  • ⚠ Buyers registering but not sending inquiries → Your product catalog is too thin or too expensive.
  • ⚠ Orders placed but cancelled frequently → Sellers are too slow to respond or prices are too high.
  • ⚠ Payments disputed frequently → Buyers and sellers don't trust the platform. Add manual verification.
