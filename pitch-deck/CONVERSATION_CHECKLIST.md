## 1. Pre-Call Preparation

### Before Every Call

| Check | Action |
|-------|--------|
| ☐ | Research the company on IndiaMart / Google / LinkedIn |
| ☐ | Know their product categories and approximate pricing |
| ☐ | Check if they already sell online (own website, Amazon, Flipkart) |
| ☐ | Have 3–5 specific product names ready to mention |
| ☐ | Know their location (city) to reference delivery range |
| ☐ | Prepare your 30-second pitch in your head |
| ☐ | Have a notepad open (or CRM) to take notes |
| ☐ | Smile before dialing — it changes your voice tone |

### Mindset

- **You are not selling a platform. You are offering a new sales channel.**
- **You are not asking for money. You are asking for their product catalog.**
- **Your goal is NOT to close on the first call. Your goal is to get a 15-minute demo or a catalog share.**

## 2. Seller Outreach Calls

### Call Opening (First 30 Seconds)

> **Goal**: Get past the "I'm not interested" reflex. Be direct, be relevant, be quick.

**Script 1 — Direct Value Opening**

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Corpass. I'm calling because 
I help suppliers like [Company Name] get corporate orders without 
chasing buyers. Do you have 90 seconds?"
```

**Script 2 — Referral Opening** (if you have a mutual connection or competitor)

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name]. I work with a few suppliers in 
[City] who sell [Product Category] to companies. I noticed you 
also carry [specific product]. Are you currently selling to 
corporate buyers directly?"
```

**Script 3 — Observation Opening**

```
"Hi [Name], I came across [Company Name] on IndiaMart. Your 
[Product] looks like a great fit for office procurement. I'm 
building a platform where corporate buyers discover vendors 
like you. Can I ask — do you currently get B2B orders online?"
```

---

### Discovery Questions (After They Say Yes)

**Question 1: Current Channel**

```
"How do corporate buyers currently find you? Is it mostly 
IndiaMart, referrals, or direct outreach?"
```
*What you're listening for*: Do they even get B2B orders? Are they frustrated with existing channels?

**Question 2: Volume**

```
"Roughly what percentage of your business is B2B vs. B2C?"
```
*What you're listening for*: If they say 80% B2C, they might not be a fit. If they say 50%+ B2B, they're a great fit.

**Question 3: Pain Point**

```
"What's the hardest part about getting repeat orders from 
corporate clients?"
```
*What you're listening for*: "They negotiate hard," "Payments are delayed," "They don't come back" — these are all Corpass solutions.

**Question 4: Catalog**

```
"Do you have a digital catalog or price list you share with 
buyers?"
```
*What you're listening for*: If they do, onboarding is easy. If they don't, you may need to help them create one.

**Question 5: GST & Compliance**

```
"Are you GST-registered? We require GSTIN for all sellers on 
the platform."
```
*What you're listening for*: If they hesitate, they might not be formally registered. This is a filter.

---

### The Pitch (2–3 Minutes)

```
"Here's what Corpass does in one sentence: We connect verified 
sellers with corporate buyers, and we handle the entire order 
workflow — inquiry, negotiation, payment tracking, and invoicing.

For you specifically, this means:

One: You list your products once. Buyers discover you through 
    search and filters. No cold calling.

Two: Buyers send inquiries through the platform. You respond 
    with a quote. Everything is tracked — no lost emails.

Three: When they place an order, you control the fulfillment. 
    Confirm, ship, deliver. Stock auto-deducts so you never 
    oversell.

Four: Payment is recorded on the platform with a UTR number. 
    Both you and the buyer have a shared record. No more 
    'Did you pay?' confusion.

Five: After delivery, they review you. Good reviews = more 
    buyers finding you.

And the pricing is simple: No listing fee. No monthly fee for 
the basic plan. We take a 3–5% commission only when a sale 
happens. You only pay when you earn."
```

---

### Ask for the Next Step

**Never ask: "Do you want to sign up?"** (Too big. Too scary.)

**Instead ask:**

```
"Would you be open to a 15-minute screen share where I show you 
how the seller dashboard works? I can walk you through listing 
your first product."
```

```
"Can you share your product catalog with me — even a PDF or 
Excel? I can upload your first 5 products to the platform myself 
so you can see how it looks."
```

```
"Are you free tomorrow at 11 AM for a quick call? I'll send you 
a link. You can see the platform, ask questions, and decide if 
it's worth 15 minutes of your time."
```

## 3. Buyer Engagement Calls

### Call Opening (First 30 Seconds)

**Script 1 — Pain Point Opening**

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Corpass. I speak with a 
lot of procurement heads, and the one thing I hear constantly is: 
'Getting quotes from 3 suppliers takes 3 days and 15 emails.' 
Does that sound familiar?"
```

**Script 2 — Direct Value Opening**

```
"Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name]. I built a platform where you can 
send one RFQ and get responses from 5 verified suppliers in 
under 24 hours. Do you have 2 minutes?"
```

**Script 3 — Referral Opening**

```
"Hi [Name], [Referral Name] from [Company] suggested I reach 
out. They mentioned you handle [Category] procurement. I'm 
working on a platform that streamlines vendor discovery and 
order tracking. Would you be open to a quick chat?"
```

---

### Discovery Questions

**Question 1: Current Process**

```
"Walk me through how you currently buy [Office Supplies / IT 
Hardware / Furniture]. Where do you find vendors?"
```
*What you're listening for*: Are they using IndiaMart? WhatsApp groups? Referrals? This tells you their sophistication level.

**Question 2: Volume & Frequency**

```
"Roughly how much does [Company] spend on [Category] annually? 
And how often do you place orders?"
```
*What you're listening for*: If they spend ₹2L+ per year, they're a great fit. If they spend ₹50K, they might be too small.

**Question 3: Pain Point**

```
"What's the most frustrating part of your procurement process 
today?"
```
*What you're listening for*: 
- "Tracking orders" → Show them order lifecycle
- "Comparing quotes" → Show them marketplace + RFQ
- "Payment reconciliation" → Show them payment trust ledger
- "Knowing what we own" → Show them asset management (this is your ace)

**Question 4: Approval Process**

```
"Who else is involved in approving a purchase? Is it just you, 
or do you need sign-off from a director?"
```
*What you're listening for*: If multiple people approve, the platform's order tracking and invoice features become more valuable.

**Question 5: Existing Vendors**

```
"Do you currently work with the same 2–3 vendors for most 
purchases, or do you shop around for every order?"
```
*What you're listening for*: If they shop around, they want discovery. If they stick to vendors, they want better tracking and asset management.

---

### The Pitch (2–3 Minutes)

```
"Corpass is a procurement platform built specifically for 
companies like yours. Here's what it does:

One: Discovery. You browse verified sellers across 12 
    categories. Each seller has a rating, GSTIN, and delivery 
    range. No more guessing if a vendor is reliable.

Two: RFQ. You send a structured inquiry — quote, feasibility, 
    or availability. Sellers respond within the platform. You 
    compare quotes side by side.

Three: Negotiation. If a quote is too high, you counter-offer. 
    The seller can accept, decline, or counter back. 
    Everything is recorded.

Four: Order Tracking. Once you place an order, you see the 
    exact status: Confirmed, Shipped, Delivered. No more 
    calling to ask 'Where is my order?'

Five: Payment Trust. You pay the seller directly via bank 
    transfer or UPI. Then you record the UTR on the platform. 
    The seller acknowledges receipt. Both of you have a 
    shared, immutable record. Zero disputes.

Six — and this is the unique part: Asset Management. Every 
    item that gets delivered is automatically logged into 
    your asset dashboard. You know what you own, where it is, 
    and what condition it's in. No more 'We bought 200 chairs 
    last year, where are they?'

It's free for buyers. We make money from the seller side."
```

---

### Ask for the Next Step

```
"Would you be open to a 15-minute demo? I'll show you the 
platform with live seller data. You can send a test inquiry and 
see how the workflow feels."
```

```
"Can I send you an invite link? You can register, browse the 
marketplace, and see if any of our sellers carry what you need. 
No commitment."
```

```
"What if I connect you with one of our sellers who specializes 
in [their category]? You can send them a real inquiry through 
the platform and see how it works."
```

## 4. Common Objections & Responses

### SELLER OBJECTIONS

---

**Objection 1: "I already get orders from IndiaMart / JustDial / my own website."**

```
RESPONSE:
"That's great — it means you already know B2B works. Corpass 
isn't replacing those channels. It's an additional channel. 
The difference is: on IndiaMart, a buyer finds you and then 
 everything happens offline. On Corpass, the entire workflow 
— inquiry, negotiation, payment, invoice — stays on the 
platform. You get structured orders, not random WhatsApp 
messages. And your reviews are tied to real transactions, not 
anonymous ratings."
```

**Objection 2: "What if the buyer doesn't pay?"**

```
RESPONSE:
"That's exactly why we built the payment trust ledger. The buyer 
records every payment with a UTR number. You acknowledge it or 
dispute it. If a buyer has a pattern of disputed payments, their 
rating drops and other sellers can see that. Plus, you control 
fulfillment — you don't ship until the advance is acknowledged. 
The platform protects both sides."
```

**Objection 3: "I don't have time to upload products."**

```
RESPONSE:
"I completely understand. Here's what I can do: send me your 
catalog — even a PDF or a price list on WhatsApp. I'll upload 
your first 5 products myself. You just log in, review them, and 
approve. 10 minutes of your time. If you like it, you add more. 
If you don't, no hard feelings."
```

**Objection 4: "3–5% commission is too high."**

```
RESPONSE:
"Fair point. Let's look at it this way: how much do you spend 
on cold calling, IndiaMart leads, or running ads? Corpass brings 
you warm buyers who already know what they want. No ad spend. No 
lead chasing. And you only pay when a sale happens. If you do 
₹1L in sales, the commission is ₹3,000–5,000. What's the cost of 
getting that same order through your current channel?"
```

**Objection 5: "I don't trust online platforms."**

```
RESPONSE:
"I understand. Most of our sellers felt the same way initially. 
Here's what I suggest: don't commit to anything. I'll upload 3 
products for you. You don't pay a rupee. When your first inquiry 
comes in, you'll see exactly how it works. If you don't like it, 
you delete the listings. Zero risk."
```

**Objection 6: "My products are custom / made-to-order. Fixed pricing doesn't work."**

```
RESPONSE:
"Perfect — that's exactly why we have two pricing modes. You 
can set your product to 'Contact for Quote' instead of 'Fixed 
Price.' Buyers must send an inquiry first. You review their 
requirements and send a custom quote through the platform. The 
negotiation happens inside Corpass, not on WhatsApp."
```

---

### BUYER OBJECTIONS

---

**Objection 1: "I already have 2–3 trusted vendors. I don't need more."**

```
RESPONSE:
"That's exactly the point. You don't need more vendors — you 
need better tracking of the ones you already use. Corpass isn't 
just about discovery. It's about order tracking, payment 
reconciliation, and asset management. Even if you stick with 
your current vendors, imagine knowing the exact status of every 
order, having a shared payment record with each vendor, and 
seeing every procured item in your asset dashboard. That's the 
value."
```

**Objection 2: "My procurement process is already set up. I don't want to change it."**

```
RESPONSE:
"I hear that a lot. Here's the thing: Corpass doesn't replace 
your process. It digitizes it. You still negotiate with the same 
vendors. You still pay them the same way. But now you have a 
single dashboard where every order, every payment, and every asset 
is tracked. When your CEO asks 'What did we spend on IT this 
quarter?' you'll have the answer in 10 seconds instead of 
digging through Excel."
```

**Objection 3: "I don't want to pay through a platform. I prefer direct bank transfer."**

```
RESPONSE:
"You still pay directly. We don't hold your money. You do a 
bank transfer or UPI to the seller exactly like you do today. 
The only difference is: after you pay, you record the UTR on 
Corpass. The seller acknowledges it. Now both of you have a 
shared record. Next month, when the accounts team asks 'Did we 
pay Vendor X?' you have proof. No more 'I think we paid' 
conversations."
```

**Objection 4: "This sounds complicated. My team isn't tech-savvy."**

```
RESPONSE:
"It's simpler than using WhatsApp for procurement. Here's the 
entire buyer flow: Search → Click Inquire → Type message → Wait 
for response → Place Order → Record Payment. That's it. If your 
team can use Amazon, they can use Corpass. And I'll personally 
walk your team through it in a 15-minute call."
```

**Objection 5: "What if the sellers on your platform are not reliable?"**

```
RESPONSE:
"Every seller is GST-verified. Their rating is based on real 
completed orders, not fake reviews. You can see their delivery 
range, their city, their company details before you even send 
an inquiry. And if a seller doesn't perform, their rating drops 
and they get fewer inquiries. The platform incentivizes good 
behavior. Plus, you're always in control — you choose who to 
work with."
```

**Objection 6: "I need to get approval from my director / CFO."**

```
RESPONSE:
"Perfect. Can I send you a one-pager and a 5-minute Loom video 
that you can forward to them? It explains the platform, the 
security, and the cost — which is zero for buyers. If they have 
questions, I'm happy to jump on a call with both of you."
```

## 5. Closing Techniques

### The "Micro-Yes" Close

Don't ask for a big commitment. Ask for a tiny one.

```
❌ WRONG: "Will you sign up today?"
✅ RIGHT: "Can I send you the invite link so you can browse 
    the marketplace?"

❌ WRONG: "Will you list all your products?"
✅ RIGHT: "Can you send me 5 product names so I can upload 
    them for you?"

❌ WRONG: "Will you switch all your procurement to Corpass?"
✅ RIGHT: "Can you send one test inquiry to see how the seller 
    responds?"
```

### The "Assumption" Close

Assume they've already decided to try it. Move to logistics.

```
"Great! I'll send you the registration link right after this 
call. What's the best email for the invite?"
```

```
"Perfect. Can you WhatsApp me your catalog PDF? I'll have your 
first 5 products live by tomorrow evening."
```

### The "Alternative" Close

Give them two options, both of which lead to a next step.

```
"Would you prefer a demo call tomorrow at 11 AM or Thursday at 
3 PM?"
```

```
"Should I upload your standard products first, or do you want 
to start with your bestsellers?"
```

### The "Loss Aversion" Close

Frame what they miss by not acting.

```
"We're onboarding our first 20 sellers with no commission for 
the first 3 orders. After that, the standard 3–5% applies. I'd 
hate for you to miss that window."
```

```
"We have 3 buyers in [City] already sending inquiries for 
[Product Category]. If you're not on the platform, they're 
going to your competitors."
```

## 6. Post-Call Follow-Up

### Immediate (Within 15 Minutes)

Send a WhatsApp or email with:

```
Subject: [Name], here's your Corpass invite link

Hi [Name],

Thanks for the call. As promised, here's your registration link:
[LINK]

For sellers: Once you log in, go to "Catalog" and click "Add 
Product." If you get stuck, reply to this message and I'll 
help.

For buyers: Browse the marketplace at [LINK]/catalog. When you 
see something interesting, click "Inquire" and the seller will 
respond directly on the platform.

I'm here if you have any questions.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Phone Number]
```

### Day 2 Follow-Up (If No Action)

```
"Hi [Name], just checking in. Did you get a chance to look at 
the platform? Happy to walk you through it on a quick call if 
it's easier."
```

### Day 5 Follow-Up (If Still No Action)

```
"Hi [Name], I noticed you haven't listed any products yet. 
Totally understand — you're busy. Want me to upload your first 
3 products for you? Just send me a PDF or even photos on 
WhatsApp. I'll handle the rest."
```

### Day 10 Follow-Up (Final Attempt)

```
"Hi [Name], I don't want to be annoying. If Corpass isn't a 
priority right now, I completely understand. I'll check back 
in a month. In the meantime, if you ever want to explore a new 
channel for corporate orders, my number is saved. Good luck 
with [Company Name]!"
```

## Quick Reference: Tone Checklist

| ✅ DO | ❌ DON'T |
|-------|----------|
| Sound like a consultant, not a salesperson | Sound desperate or pushy |
| Ask questions 70% of the time, talk 30% | Pitch for 10 minutes without pausing |
| Use their language ("procurement," "fulfillment") | Use jargon they don't understand |
| Admit what you don't know | Overpromise features that don't exist yet |
| Offer to do the work for them (upload products) | Ask them to do heavy lifting upfront |
| Follow up consistently but not aggressively | Call every day until they answer |
| Reference real sellers/buyers already on the platform | Make up numbers or testimonials |

## Call Scorecard (Rate Yourself After Each Call)

| Criteria | Score (1–5) |
|----------|-------------|
| Did I research the company before calling? | |
| Did I ask at least 3 discovery questions? | |
| Did I listen more than I talked? | |
| Did I identify a specific pain point? | |
| Did I connect that pain point to a Corpass feature? | |
| Did I ask for a micro-commitment (not a big one)? | |
| Did I send a follow-up within 15 minutes? | |
| Did I set a specific next step with a date/time? | |

**Total / 40 = your call quality score**

- 35–40: Excellent. This will convert.
- 25–34: Good. Minor tweaks needed.
- 15–24: Okay. You pitched too much, listened too little.
- Below 15: Bad. Go back to the discovery questions.
